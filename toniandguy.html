The Challenge

A leaky bucket across 16 stores.

state before intervention
01

Fragmented execution.

No central playbook. Each store improvised its own marketing in isolation, creating inconsistent messaging and wasted spend with no network-level learning.

02

Poor creative-audience fit.

CPC at ₹14+. CTR near 1%. Low quality scores punished every auction. Generic creative served to generic audiences produced generic, expensive results.

03

No CRM infrastructure.

Customer data scattered across booking sheets and WhatsApp threads. No unified view. No segmentation. No way to identify high-value customers or act on behaviour.

04

27% customer churn.

Customers vanished after one visit. No lifecycle, no re-engagement, no loyalty architecture. The acquisition machine was filling a bucket with a hole in the bottom.

The Strategy

The Growth Ecosystem.

six interlocked pillars
PILLAR 01 · ACQUIRE

Google Ads, PMax & Meta.

112+ creatives tested. Store-level targeting with postcode precision. PMax campaigns for booking intent; Meta for awareness and retargeting at location level.

PILLAR 02 · EXPERIENCE

Website CRO & booking funnel.

Mobile app optimisation, booking funnel A/B testing, landing page restructure per store. Every conversion point pressure-tested against the data.

PILLAR 03 · CONTENT & BRAND

100+ monthly assets.

Influencer programme, PR pipeline, Google Review reputation management across 16 listings. Brand consistency enforced at every customer touchpoint.

PILLAR 04 · ENGAGE & RETAIN

WhatsApp, email, push, loyalty.

Churn reduced from 27% → 7%. Win-back flows, birthday triggers, tier-based loyalty mechanics. Re-engagement sequences tied to visit recency and service history.

PILLAR 05 · CUSTOMER DATA

HubSpot / Zoho CRM.

Segmentation by service type, visit frequency, location, spend. Lead scoring for salon upgrade journeys. First unified customer record across 16 stores.

PILLAR 06 · MEASURE

GA4, GTM & Looker Studio.

ROAS, CPA, CPL measured per store. Custom Looker Studio dashboards. Weekly performance briefs with anomaly detection. Every channel justified by its own P&L.

Key Learnings

What I'd tell another marketer.

distilled from 16 months
01

Granularity beats averages.

Treating 16 stores as one campaign hides winners and losers. Per-store spend revealed where to refresh creative vs. shift budget. A city-wide average would have hidden that the bottom 8 stores need entirely different creative and budget logic than the flagships.

02

CTR leads, CPC follows.

Once creative-audience fit improved (CTR up 5×), CPC collapsed — Google rewards relevance with cheaper auctions. The work was not on bids. It was on understanding which creative, which audience, which offer, at which store, at which moment. Then systematising that understanding across 112+ creatives.

03

Retention beats acquisition.

Cutting churn from 27% → 7% was worth more than any single paid optimisation. Every percentage point of retention improvement compounds forward. Lifecycle is leverage. The acquisition engine was filling a bucket with a hole in the bottom; we plugged the hole first.

04

CRM is infrastructure, not a tool.

Without centralised data, every channel flies blind. CRM is priority #1 in any multi-location B2C. The HubSpot / Zoho implementation was the unlock for everything else — segmentation, lead scoring, lifecycle automation, attribution, and the weekly executive brief that ran decisions faster.
