## 🎯 The Challenge

TONI&GUY — operating **16 salons across Bengaluru** — needed to transform fragmented, store-level marketing into a unified growth engine.

**Problems on arrival:**
- Marketing was reactive and uncoordinated across stores
- High CPC (₹14+) with low CTR (~1%) signalling poor creative-audience fit
- No CRM or lifecycle automation — repeat bookings depended on memory and goodwill
- No unified attribution between paid spend, walk-ins, and revenue
- Customer churn at **27%** — a leaky bucket

---

## 🏗️ The Strategy: A 6-Pillar Growth Ecosystem

Designed and executed a complete growth system across **six pillars**:

### 1. ACQUIRE (Traffic Generation)
- **Performance Marketing** — Google Ads, Performance Max, Demand Gen
- **Meta Advertising** — Facebook + Instagram, 112+ active creatives
- **SEO & Organic Visibility** — Local SEO, service page optimization
- **Social Media Organic** — Reels, stories, brand content

### 2. DIGITAL EXPERIENCE
- Website + Shopify optimization
- Mobile app (launched on Play Store)
- Booking flow conversion optimization

### 3. CONTENT & BRAND
- 100+ monthly content assets
- Influencer & PR partnerships
- Reputation management across listings

### 4. ENGAGE & RETAIN
- WhatsApp marketing (10 campaigns/month, 5,000+ customer database)
- Email automation, push notifications, RCS messaging
- Loyalty & rewards program

### 5. CUSTOMER DATA PLATFORM
- HubSpot/Zoho CRM as central spine
- Cross-channel data integration (Google Ads, Meta, WhatsApp, Email)
- Customer journey mapping & lead scoring

### 6. MEASURE · ANALYZE · OPTIMIZE
- GA4 + GTM full-funnel tracking
- Looker Studio executive dashboards
- ROAS, CPA, CPL tracking per store

## ⚙️ Execution Highlights

### Paid Media Optimisation
Built **store-level campaign architecture** — every one of the 16 stores got its own performance lens, not a generic city-wide blast.

- **112+ active ads** running monthly
- **₹3.5L – ₹3.8L** monthly Google Ads budget
- **₹1.4L – ₹1.6L** monthly Meta Ads budget
- Audience retargeting + lookalike expansion
- Conversion tracking & attribution per store

### The CPC Story
> Aggressive creative testing, keyword refinement, and audience segmentation drove **CPC down from ₹14.23 (Jan 2025) to ₹4.70 (Apr 2026)** — a **67% reduction** while scaling spend.

### The CTR Story
> Creative refresh cycles and intent-based targeting drove **CTR from 1.39% to a peak of 5.24%** — a **276% improvement**, signalling massive lift in ad-audience fit.

### CRM & Retention Architecture
- Built segmentation by visit frequency, service type, and lifetime value
- Trigger-based lifecycle journeys (welcome, post-visit, win-back)
- Reduced churn from **27% → 7%**

## 💡 Key Lessons

1. **Store-level granularity beats city-level averages.** Treating 16 stores as one campaign hides winners and losers. Separating spend per store revealed which locations needed creative refreshes vs. budget shifts.

2. **CTR is a leading indicator; CPC follows.** Once creative-audience fit improved (CTR up 5x), CPC collapsed naturally because Google rewards relevance.

3. **Acquisition without retention is a leaky bucket.** Cutting churn from 27% → 7% was worth more than any single paid channel optimisation.

4. **CRM is infrastructure, not a tool.** Without centralised customer data, every channel is flying blind. CRM should be priority #1 in any multi-location B2C.
