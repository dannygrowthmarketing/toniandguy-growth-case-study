Flagship malls anchor the network

Phoenix MarketCity (₹49.9M) and Phoenix Mall of Asia (₹46.2M) alone drive 34% of revenue — high-footfall locations where premium positioning converts.

New stores ramped fast

The acquisition playbook transferred cleanly: Koramangala 4th Block went 0→298 monthly orders in 9 months; Vega City hit 321 in just 4 months from launch.

Granularity surfaced the truth

A city-wide average would hide that the bottom 8 stores need different creative and budget logic than the flagships. Per-store P&Ls made every decision sharper.

Key Learnings

What I'd tell another marketer

Distilled from 16 months of testing, tracking, iterating.

01
Granularity beats averages

Treating 16 stores as one campaign hides winners and losers. Per-store spend revealed where to refresh creative vs. shift budget.

02
CTR leads, CPC follows

Once creative-audience fit improved (CTR up 5x), CPC collapsed — Google rewards relevance with cheaper auctions.

03
Retention beats acquisition

Cutting churn 27%→7% was worth more than any single paid optimisation. Lifecycle is leverage.

04
CRM is infrastructure

Without centralised data, every channel flies blind. CRM is priority #1 in any multi-location B2C.
